Chapter 1: Description of Data

Introduction

Data refers to raw facts and observations collected from experiments, surveys, or real-life situations. The main objective of statistics is to organize, analyze, and interpret data in order to draw meaningful conclusions and support decision-making.

Types of Variables

Variables represent characteristics that can take different values. They are classified into the following types:

  • Qualitative (Categorical): Non-numerical data such as gender, color, or category.
  • Quantitative: Numerical data which can be:
    • Discrete: Countable values (e.g., number of students).
    • Continuous: Measured values (e.g., height, weight).

Mean and Weighted Mean

The mean is the arithmetic average of a data set and provides a measure of central tendency.

The weighted mean is used when different data values have different levels of importance, where weights are assigned accordingly.

Median and Mode

The median is the middle value when data is arranged in ascending or descending order.

The mode is the value that occurs most frequently in the data set.

Quartiles

Quartiles divide the data into four equal parts: Q1 (lower quartile), Q2 (median), and Q3 (upper quartile). They help in understanding the spread and distribution of data.

Variance

Variance measures the degree of dispersion of data points from the mean. A higher variance indicates that data points are more spread out.

Coefficient of Variation

The coefficient of variation (CV) is a relative measure of dispersion. It is expressed as:
CV = (Standard Deviation / Mean) × 100

Skewness

Skewness describes the symmetry of a data distribution. A distribution can be positively skewed, negatively skewed, or symmetric.

Histogram

A histogram is a graphical representation of frequency distribution using bars. It helps visualize the shape and spread of data.

Box Plot

A box plot summarizes data using minimum value, quartiles, median, and maximum value. It is useful for detecting outliers.
